              Types of Mead

              Traditional mead is a relatively simple alcoholic beverage made from honey, water and yeast. There are numerous named variations of mead from all over the world. Here's a partial list of the various kinds of mead:

              Acerglyn
              Made with honey and maple syrup
              Bilbemel
              Made with honey and blueberries or blueberry juice
              Black Mead
              Mead made from honey and blackcurrants
              Bochet
              Mead made from caramelized honey
              Bochetomel
              Mead made from caramelized honey and fruits like elderberries, raspberries and/or blackberries
              Braggot
              Mead made from honey, hops and/or malt
              Capsicumel
              Mead made from honey and peppers (mild or hot)
              Coffeemel
              Made with honey and cold-brewed coffee
              Cyser
              Made with honey and apple juice
              Hydromel
              Light or low-alcohol mead
              Melomel
              Made with honey and fruit
              Metheglin
              Mead made from honey with herbs or spices added, like nutmeg, coriander, cinnamon, cloves or vanilla
              Morat
              Mead made from honey and mulberries
              Mulled Mead
              Made with honey and spices like ginger, cloves and/or cinnamon and served warm
              Oxymel
              Blend of honey and wine vinegar
              Pyment
              Made from honey and a blend of grapes
              Rhodomel
              Made with honey, rose hips, rose petal or rose attar

              Mead is an ancient beverage that has been part of the culture of various societies around the world for thousands of years. Use the link below to learn more about mead at Wikipedia.

              ABV Formula

              Mead Mate supports four common Alcohol By Volume (ABV) formulas. The Standard formula is the default.

              Standard Formula:
              (OG - FG) * 131.25
              Common formula for calculating ABV.

              High Standard Formula:
              (OG - FG) * 135
              Formula preferred by YouTube channel 'CS Mead and More' for ABV values higher than 7%.

              Alternate Formula:
              Provided by Dr. Michael Hall
              Tends to land between the Standard and High Standard simplified formulas and considered to be the most accurate.

              OE = –668.962 + 1262.45 OG – 776.43 OG^2 + 182.94 OG^3
              AE = –668.962 + 1262.45 FG – 776.43 FG^2 + 182.94 FG^3
              q = 0.22 + 0.001 OE
              RE = (q OE + AE) / (1 + q)
              A%w = (OE – RE) / (2.0665 – 0.010665 OE)
              A%v = A%w (FG / 0.794)

              Alternate formula for ABV values.

              Wine Formula:
              (OG - FG) / 7.36 * 1000
              Formula commonly associated with homebrewing wine.

              Mead Mate Release 11

              With this release, the Original Specific Gravity reading on a new batch is no longer required. This will prevent the app from showing the ABV of the batch, but should be more friendly to new mead makers that don't have a hydrometer yet.

              What's New

              • Expanded allowed range for gravity readings to 0.700 to 2.000
              • The Original Gravity field for a batch is no longer required
              • Minor tweaks to the logging in the data layer
